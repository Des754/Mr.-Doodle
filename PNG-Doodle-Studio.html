Bob Browne Style Guide

✏️ Bold ink outlines — thick, confident strokes. Characters have weight.

😄 Exaggerated expressions — round heads, wide eyes, big mouths showing shock, joy, frustration.

🎨 Flat colour fills — no gradients. Strong, saturated fills inside clean outlines.

🗨️ Handwritten Tok Pisin bubbles — speech and thought bubbles with pidgin text.

🌴 PNG props — bilum bags, kundu drums, PMV buses, kaukau, thatch roofs, coconut palms.

TTS Export Guide

ElevenLabs (recommended)
Use a warm baritone (male) or alto (female). Set stability: 0.4, similarity: 0.75. Speaking rate: 0.82 for Melanesian cadence.

Google Cloud TTS
Use en-AU-Neural2-B (male) or en-AU-Neural2-C (female) — closest to PNG English intonation. Add SSML <break> after Tok Pisin phrases.

Amazon Polly
Use Matthew (male) or Joanna (female) with SSML <prosody rate="slow" pitch="+2st"> to simulate PNG cadence.

Bob Browne Drawing Notes — Character Design

Each character in the Grasruts tradition has distinct visual hallmarks that animators and illustrators should follow:

  • Mista Grasruts — scruffy beard, worn shorts, singlet, thongs. Always slightly stooped forward. Cigarette optional. Large expressive eyes. Usually mid-scheme or mid-scheme-gone-wrong face.
  • Agnes — strong build, laplap skirt, hair pulled back. Frequently holds the famous sospen (saucepan). Expression ranges from stern to exasperated to quietly knowing.
  • Big Fella Chief — oversized suit jacket (too big), briefcase, hat. Pompous posture. Large belly. Wide smile hiding shifty eyes.
  • Pikinini — small, barefoot, shorts too big. Constantly curious expression. Often asking the question adults won't.
  • Tisa — neat dress or blouse, glasses, holds chalk or book. Patient expression. Warm smile. The voice of reason.
  • Maket Meri — bilum bag on back, market stall context, bilum on head. Assertive, confident stance. Practical clothing.
  • Highlander — traditional wig, koteka, body paint red and black. Strong stance. Sometimes fish-out-of-water in urban settings — great for culture-clash comedy.
